Characters, Equipment, and Cities

  • Character: This is central table of the Superhero database. It contains entries for all heroes and villains within the database, those heroes' real first and last names, their cities and references to their mentors (if they have one). The "role" boolean value also indicates whether the character is considered a hero or villain.
  • Equipment: The Equipment table contains the weapons, armor, costumes, and other key pieces of equipment for the characters in the Character table. Each piece of equipment has a specific name, a more generic description ("hammer," "bow," etc.), a material, and a reference to its owning character. A piece of equipment MUST have an owner, and if the owning character's row is deleted from the Character table, that piece of equipment will be deleted, as well.
  • Cities: The Cities table contains all of the cities, real and fictional, in which the characters live. These cities can be real or fictional, indicated by a boolean value in the table. For instance, Gotham is a fictional city where Batman lives, and New York City is a real city where Spiderman lives. A character's city reference is allowed to be null in the Character table, and a city may exist in the Cities table without any characters' city references currently pointing to it.

Powers and Weaknesses

  • Powers: The Powers table contains all of the characters' superpowers. Each power includes a type (super strength, for instance) and a magnitude on a scale from 1 to 10.
  • Character Powers: Powers exist in a many-to-many relationship with Characters. For instance, there may be multiple characters with super strength of magnitude 7. Thus, this table exists to clarify the relationships between characters and their respective powers.
  • Weaknesses: The Weaknesses table is analogous to the Powers table, but for weaknesses. Each weakness includes a type (Kryptonite, for instance) and a magnitude from 1 to 10.
  • Character Weaknesses: Like Powers, Weaknesses exist in a many-to-many relationship with Characters. For instance, both Superman and Supergirl might have a Kryptonite weakness of magnitude 10. Thus, this table exists to clarify the relationships between characters and their respective strengths.

Friendships and Rivalries

  • Friend Relationships: Characters can be friends with other characters, and because any given character may be friends with 0, 1 or many other characters, this is a many-to-many relationship. Heroes are allowed to be friends with villains, and vice-versa. Duplicate entries are not allowed (e.g. if Batman is friends with Superman, there will not be an entry that also says Superman is friends with Batman).
  • Rival Relationships: Characters can also be rivals with other characters, and this is likewise a many-to-many relationship. Heroes can be considered rivals with other heroes, and villains may be considered rivals with other villains (e.g. Cyclops and Wolverine kind of hate each other). Like the Friend Relationship table, this table prevents what essentially amount to duplicate entries (if Cyclops is a rival to Wolverine, there will not be an entry that says Wolverine is a rival to Cyclops.)
